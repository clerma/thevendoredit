Your wedding photographs are the one thing you'll have long after the flowers fade and the cake is gone. Choosing the right photographer isn't just about finding someone with a good camera — it's about finding someone whose eye, style, and personality are the right fit for you.

## Start with Style

Wedding photography comes in many styles — editorial, documentary, film-inspired, traditional, and everything in between. Before you start reaching out to photographers, spend time on Instagram and wedding blogs identifying images that consistently stop you mid-scroll. That's your style.

## Look Beyond the Highlights Reel

Any photographer can put together a beautiful portfolio of hero shots. Ask to see full wedding galleries — beginning to end. This shows you how they handle the quiet moments between the big ones, and whether the quality holds up throughout a full day.

## Meet Before You Book

Chemistry matters. You'll spend eight to ten hours with your photographer on your wedding day. They need to be someone you genuinely feel comfortable with, because that ease shows in the photos.

## Questions to Ask

- How many weddings do you photograph per year?
- Will you be the photographer on our day, or will you send an associate?
- How do you handle low-light situations (church ceremonies, evening receptions)?
- What is your turnaround time for delivery?
- Do you carry backup equipment?

## Don't Let Price Be the Only Factor

We understand budgets are real. But photography is one area where the investment pays dividends for the rest of your life. If you need to trim somewhere, start with the floral centerpieces — not the photographer.
